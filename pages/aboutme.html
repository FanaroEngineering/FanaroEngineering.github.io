About Me

Born (1992) and raised in São Paulo, Brazil, I graduated at the beginning of 2017 in Electrical/Telecommunications Engineering at the Polytechnic School of the University of São Paulo. During my bachelor’s studies, I had the privilege of participating in the Science without Borders exchange program of the Federal Government. Therefore, for a year, I’ve lived abroad — more specifically, in Brussels, Belgium — studying engineering and, in parallel, improving my French and my abilities in Go (Baduk or Weiqi).

In 2017, after 5 years of love for the board game of Go — an Asian strategy game, analogous to chess both in prestige and complexity —, I had the opportunity of to improve my skills and, also, study topics related to the game, a very enriching experience through which I’ve come to know more about the surprisingly different ways of Asian thinking and behavior.

In 2018, after a semester of full-time studies for jobs in the government, especially for the Federal Police, , more specifically in the Machine Learning field, a component of the greater area of Data Science. I spent the rest of that year rehabilitating myself as a software developer, studying statistics, practicing my newly (re)developed Data Science skills and looking for a job in this new field.

In 2019, in February, I managed to get an opportunity at well established retail software company in Brazil: Zanthus. I and another colleague were supposed to create the new Data Science department and, hopefully, instill cultural changes in the corporation also. The available data wasn’t very clean or organized — besides the natural lack of a data-driven structure, since they couldn’t expect it to be a need years ago — and, therefore, it was very difficult to make anything work at the beginning. After around 3-4 months of exploration, some successful algorithms were developed and I was then charged with the development of the future data API that would take care of the back end of the future data-related products. After 4 more months of development, at the beginning of October, now with a functional — only for internal tests — minimal API (using the Django REST Framework and Kubernetes), I realized I had experienced a typical case of burnout, while disagreements with managers were stressing me even further.
