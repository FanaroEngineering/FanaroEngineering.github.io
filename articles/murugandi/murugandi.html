The design above, called Fighting Spirit, by Murugandi, features a very famous ancient Chinese game: Liang Weijin (White) vs Fan Xiping (Black). This is the description on Etsy:

Kiai! Originally an illustration to the French book "Les Chants de Maldoror" written by Le Comte de Lautréamont (1868), pseudonym of Isidore Ducasse. In the book the main character Maldoror takes on the form of a giant eagle to fight God, who is half dragon-tiger, half serpent. The board position is from a game from the 18th century between legendary go player Fan Xiping (black) and Liang Weijin (white), known as "nailing down the guoshou title".

Ever since buying the print design, I've been planning on creating a review for this, or at least mostly transcribing a professional review somewhere. However, so far, I haven't been able to find a professional review anywhere. So I had to resort to my 1-2d KGS monkey brain. Luckily, nowadays, we can also use super strong AIs to help us, and that's exactly what I did: besides my first unbiased review, I've used Leela Zero Big (ELO 16190 at 2k Variations) to help me specially with the more complicated moves.

It should also be noted that having White start and no komi for Black really does distort Leela's plays, specially at near the end of the game. Leela might choose inferior moves because it wrongly thinks the advantage is bigger than it really is.

Another quite helpful resource was Murugandi's review of Fan Xiping vs Shi Xiangxia on his channel, which I think covers very well many of the more complicated parts of a game that is even more complicated than the one that's the focus of this post.
