I used to laugh at Twitter and its users. The concept of trying to discuss under the limitation of 280 words per tweet seemed ludicrous to me. And it still does actually.

However, now, after more careful deliberation, I can recognize two very powerful aspects of the app: sharing and connecting.

ColdFusion made a very comprehensive and jaw-dropping video on Twitter, which you can check out here. In that video, he mentions that the initial turning point for Twitter was realizing its potential as a news platform.

Due to the limitations of size, those who decide to share information there will inevitably have to use much more concise and summarized words. Thus, Twitter ends up being probably the biggest redirecting resource on the internet right now — excepting Google, of course —, because people solve the message length limitiations by sharing links to where a more in-depth discussion of the topic can be achieved. And this is actually great, it is in fact an amazing alternative approach to indexing the internet and human thought, very different from classical indexers such as Google: who wouldn't want to have a list of summaries and snippets created by their favorite authors and thinkers? — This sort of credibility does make a difference.

You may still not think that the idea is that good, but it has been very effective. It's one of the oldest and biggest modern digital media companies today, with more than 13 years of existence — founded in 2006. And, despite the sharing, the main reason for its success, in my opinion, is, in fact, the connecting. Through Twitter, you will be able to mention your personal idols and icons, and they will listen! Ok, if they are too famous, they might not, but you'll be surprised to know that they usually want to: it's good for their publicity, and they're humans too! They do want human contact, they want to talk about stuff, they do have interests. In my case, for example, I have managed to be able to get responses from very famous experts in my professional field who I would have never been able to reach otherwise. This connecting effect is so powerful that, surprisingly, even experts from lofty fields such as Data Science and Machine Learning use it as a tool of keeping up with their peers and sharing content.

Recently, after finding out about all this, I've decided to (re)enter Twitter with a more specific purpose than simply spewing out whatever came to my mind. I'm gonna try to make my account and the platform more useful to anybody — even for myself, since it will act as a registry and a timeline of my own thoughts —, I'll share only things I deem worthy of anyone's time, stuff I'm learning about everyday and find enlightening, charming, educational. I've shared some of my tweets in this post which, hopefully, will convince you that my tweets may indeed be worthwhile or, at the very least, interesting.

I think that an improved and more accurate version of this saying would be like:
