My First Logo and Its Formula

Strictly speaking, this isn't really 100% my invention. I've got 90% of the inspiration from the Anti Hentai Hentai Club T-Shirt. I still don't know the true origin of the logo, but, from watching Ricky and Elliot's Weekly Weird News shows, I'm almost convinced it comes from some anime.

You can make your own version of this T-Shirt too, you will only need to replace the "bullshit" or "hentai" for something that is bad, but, when taken jokingly, lightly or unseriously, is at least marginally positive. In the end, this logo is probably one of the most elementary templates for an oxymoron, so you can look for books or articles on this type of figure of speech if you want to conceive a better, literary version of the logo.

\[ Oxymoronic \ Logo = \textbf{ANTI} \ \texttt{<concept>} \ \texttt{<concept>} \ \textbf{CLUB} \]

With respect to the font, I'm not 100% sure what the original is. Using Adobe Capture's Type functionality, the closest I could get was Alverata Informal Black. The original designers also seem to have deconstructed the fonts in order to bend and warp the letters, which I consciously chose to not do.

I'm sharing my Adobe Illustrator file so you may stamp your own T-Shirt if you wish to — just give me a shoutout on Twitter for recognition if you like the design. I chose the Philosopher Bold font for the end design; you can use Adobe's Creative Cloud to download it, or, alternatively, you can use the deconstructed version I left in the left-most, final canvas inside the .ai file.
