"Less Code" Print Design

The quote in the design is heavily inspired by StackExchange user quentin-starin's answer:

How many lines of code can a C# developer produce per month?

If they are good, less than zero.

I hope he won't mind me stealing his quote, but I'm not earning a dime from it anyway. It isn't a direct translation either, I ended up adapting it.

At first I wanted to put the quote and, right below it, a snippet of the dreaded "Hello World" converted into a design pattern. But, after seing The Futur's prints, I settled for something much simpler, and, perhaps, more elegant. The font I used is Futura PT, and it is freely available on Adobe Illustrator.

The thumbnail of this article is getting cropped and I can't do much about it, or at least I don't care to. The real designs are below and they feature variations of two characteristics: having labels/watermarks; and having different proportions, either squared, 2x3 or 3x2. You can download everything here, including the SVGs and the AI file.

The text is in pure white, however, it's considered better practice to use light grey instead because the contrast is usually too high. In The Futur's prints, I think the background is a slab of stone, so it actually looks kind of cool to have pure white. Anyway, if you would like to change the color, feel free to open the AI file and edit it yourself.
